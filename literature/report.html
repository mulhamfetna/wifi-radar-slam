1Executive summary

Four mature but historically separate communities each supply one capability this project needs. Every capability has been demonstrated — but never in this combination, and never on a moving vehicle outdoors.

outdoor / vehicular WiFi 3D-scan systems

Passive WiFi radar is mature — ambient APs as illuminators, through-wall detection, sub-metre tracking — but always indoor and static. CSI sensing reaches dense 3D reconstruction (skeletons, multi-person pose comparable to mmWave radar, depth images) but is confined to 2–4 m fixed indoor zones that generalize poorly. RF-SLAM already provides a transferable back-end (multipath-as-virtual- transmitter). The ISAC / 802.11bf / mmWave thread — which decides whether commodity WiFi can reach radar-grade resolution — is the frontier and needs a second research pass.

2The novelty gap

Defensible contribution

An on-vehicle, mobile, outdoor WiFi (802.11) passive-radar / CSI system that produces 3D scans usable inside a SLAM pipeline — combining the automotive moving-platform concept (so far shown only with 5G) with WiFi-band sensing (so far shown only indoors and static). No prior work occupies that intersection.

The closest prior art — a passive radar on a moving car (Maksymiuk 2024) — proves the moving-platform concept is real, but uses 5G downlink, not WiFi. Substituting an ambient-WiFi illuminator, on a vehicle, outdoors, with a SLAM-facing 3D output, is the unoccupied point in the design space. The novelty is the transfer of indoor/static WiFi sensing into the mobile/outdoor regime, and the engineering that transfer requires is the paper's technical content.

Two guardrails (from refuted claims)

Do not claim WiFi's low bandwidth restricts passive radar to Doppler-only — cross-ambiguity recovers full range–Doppler; bandwidth limits range resolution only. Do not attribute the automotive gap to any survey — it is our inference from the pervasive indoor/static pattern.

4Thread 1 — Passive WiFi radar · mature

The classical-radar analogue: ambient WiFi APs as illuminators of opportunity, reference + surveillance channels, cross-ambiguity processing for range/Doppler. If a car already carries a WiFi antenna, the reception hardware for a passive radar is largely present.

FindingConf.
First through-wall detection of moving personnel; extracts both range and Doppler, matching bistatic theory. (Chetty, Smith & Woodbridge, UCL, 2012) High
Ordinary 802.11b beacons are viable illuminators; beacon ambiguity function analyzed from real data. (Pham Duc Su, 2021) Medium
Fourier processing is inadequate for WiFi's short bursts / low bandwidth → ESPRIT super-resolution. (Yildirim, Griffiths, UCL, 2021) High
Runs against an unmodified stand-alone AP (CAF + modified CAF for idle beacons); receiver still a dedicated SDR. (Li, Chetty, UCL/Bristol, 2021) High
2025 CSI-power bistatic tracking — phase-independent |CSI|², cascaded 3D-FFT + EKF; ~0.4 m median indoor error, <2 ms latency. (arXiv:2511.22144) High

Takeaway. The physics and processing are solved for the indoor, static case, and we inherit a clear recipe (super-resolution front end; CAF variants; CSI-power tracking; EKF back-end). The frontier is whether it survives a moving receiver — untested here.

Load-bearing prior art

Maksymiuk et al. (2024) — a passive radar on a moving vehicle imaging its surroundings, validated on real data — but using 5G downlink. This is the exact scope of our novelty: do it with WiFi.

5Thread 2 — CSI sensing, imaging & 3D · proven but brittle

Evidence that WiFi yields the dense, structured output a SLAM front end wants — and, in the same papers, evidence of the gap.

FindingConf.
WiFi-band signals pass through walls / reflect off bodies; deep nets recover 3D skeletons ~4 cm (RF-Pose3D, MIT). FMCW radio, not 802.11 CSI. High
Commodity-WiFi 3D multi-person pose — 91.7 / 108.1 / 125.3 mm (1/2/3 persons), "comparable to cameras and mmWave radar." (Person-in-WiFi 3D, CVPR 2024) High
Depth images from CSI (Wi-Depth, 2025) and CSI→diffusion imagery (LatentCSI, 2025). High
The gap, quantified: 4×3.5 m and 2×4 m fixed indoor rigs; untrained-scene collapse to mIoU 0.12 / mPCK 19.34 (only 0.24 / 31.06 with domain adaptation). High

Takeaway. Dense reconstruction from commodity WiFi is settled; the uniform limitation is small fixed indoor zones and poor generalization. A moving vehicle is the ultimate stress test — every frame is a new environment, a reframing that may itself be a contribution.

6Thread 3 — RF-SLAM · back-end ready

FindingConf.
Channel-SLAM: each multipath component treated as a "virtual transmitter," jointly estimating VT positions with receiver pose/velocity/clock bias — no prior map, no known reflectors. Directly reusable back-end. (Gentner, DLR, 2016/2017) High
Joint comms + SLAM is explicitly "in its infancy." (Karagiannidis et al. survey, 2026) — does not name an automotive gap. High

Takeaway. We don't invent a SLAM formulation — the virtual- transmitter model is proven and citable. Open question: does it survive a fast-moving vehicular receiver and dynamic outdoor multipath? Likely the project's core research risk.

7Thread 4 — ISAC / 802.11bf / mmWave · verified (Round 2)

Decisive result — answers the core feasibility question

Commodity sub-7 GHz 802.11 CSI cannot reach automotive-radar-grade range resolution. It is bandwidth-limited (ΔR = c/2B); only 60 GHz mmWave (802.11ad/ay DMG) closes the gap — an ~11× jump.

Waveform / bandwidthRange resolution ΔR = c/2B
40 MHz (commodity 802.11n/ac channel)~3.75 m
160 MHz (802.11ac/ax/be bonded)~0.94 m
1.76 GHz (802.11ad 60 GHz DMG channel)~8.5 cm
77 GHz automotive radar (reference)~4–15 cm
FindingConf.
Range resolution is bandwidth-limited — sub-7 GHz WiFi "cannot achieve cm-level range … due to insufficiently low bandwidth." (Kumari/Heath; 802.11bf overview) High
802.11ad 60 GHz reused as a long-range radar via the preamble's Golay sequences: <0.1 m range res, <0.6 m/s velocity res, >99.9% detection to 200 m + Gbps comms. Theory/simulation, not hardware. (Kumari, Choi, González-Prelcic & Heath, TVT 2018) High
Same waveform does radar IMAGING (ISAR) in V2I — RSU transmits 802.11ad, forms high-res vehicle images from echoes. (Han, Choi & Heath, 2022) High
802.11bf standardizes the measurement plumbing, not a sensing outcome — leaves algorithms to vendors (that vendor-open space is our contribution's home). (ComST 2024) High
ISAC theory bounds the design — dual-functional shared hardware/spectrum + the fundamental sensing–communication tradeoff (CRB-rate region). (Liu 2022; Xiong/Liu 2023) High
The central design fork this forces

(1) Stay sub-7 GHz — lean on channel bonding (→ ~0.94 m at 160 MHz) and reframe the contribution as coarse occupancy + SLAM-pose mapping, where multipath geometry, not fine range, carries the information (cf. Channel-SLAM). Do not claim radar-grade resolution. (2) Move to 60 GHz / 802.11bf DMG — adopt the 802.11ad Golay-preamble radar approach to claim genuine ~8.5 cm resolution, at the cost of 60 GHz range/penetration limits and sparser ambient illumination. State this fork explicitly in the paper.

8Feasibility & risk register

  • Ego-motion & dynamic outdoor multipath (highest risk). All pipelines were validated on static indoor geometry. A moving vehicle adds ego-Doppler, moving clutter, and shifting illuminator geometry. Prototype Channel-SLAM robustness to a fast receiver first.
  • Range resolution vs bandwidth (RESOLVED — Round 2). Sub-7 GHz WiFi is bandwidth-limited to ~0.94–3.75 m; radar-grade (~8.5 cm) needs 60 GHz mmWave. Forces the design fork in §7. (Coarse resolution ≠ no range.)
  • Illumination budget outdoors. Ambient APs are sparse, low-power, uncontrolled — a link- budget question to answer analytically before building hardware.
  • Phase instability. Adopt self-conjugate |CSI|²; open question whether power-only processing retains enough information for metric 3D mapping.

10Claims that failed verification

❌ "WiFi's 20–40 MHz bandwidth means passive WiFi radar can extract only Doppler, not range." False — cross-ambiguity yields full range–Doppler surfaces. Bandwidth limits range resolution, not range extraction.

❌ "The comms-meets-SLAM survey identifies a specific automotive gap." It does not. The automotive gap is our inference — argue it ourselves.
