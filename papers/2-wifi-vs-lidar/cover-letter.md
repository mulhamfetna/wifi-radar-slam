**Fit and contribution.** The manuscript asks a question IoT-J is the natural venue for:
can the WiFi radio already present in a vehicle — sensing ambient signals from
infrastructure that already exists — replace the LiDAR that dominates its sensing bill?
We answer it with, to our knowledge, the first **on-vehicle, outdoor, head-to-head**
comparison of WiFi and LiDAR SLAM, evaluated on the same ray-traced scenes, against the
same ground truth, with the same six metrics, and with the LiDAR back-end externally
validated on real KITTI data (≈0.3 % drift).

The answer is a boundary rather than a yes or a no, and the boundary is the contribution:

- **Localization: parity, at ~1 % of the price.** Commodity-CSI WiFi matches our geometric
  LiDAR model in sparse multipath (0.098 ± 0.028 m vs 0.102 m) and is decimetre-class in
  general — at **84–600× lower sensor cost**. We are careful *not* to claim superiority.
- **Mapping: outright failure.** Occupancy IoU ≈ 0 against up to 1.00 for LiDAR. No price
  buys coverage the sensor cannot produce; the cost-normalized mapping cost is literally
  infinite.
- **The mapping ceiling, located.** An isolation experiment shows the floor is dominated by
  **phantom detections (≈89 % of MUSIC detections match no real propagation path)** and an
  estimator **range bias** (6.45 m median — far beyond the 0.94 m resolution limit, hence a
  bias and not a resolution bound), *not* by path discrimination. Consequently a learned
  discriminator cannot repair the map, and we show a heuristic, a random forest and a neural
  network failing identically. This is a **negative result reported as a result**, together
  with the mechanism that explains it.
- **A cost analysis and a fusion verdict.** Adding WiFi to an existing LiDAR costs ≈0.5 %
  more and improves localization 36–79 % — *provided the sensors are of comparable
  accuracy*; a badly mismatched pair degrades the stronger one by 8×. We report that
  regression rather than tuning it away.

**Points I would like to flag transparently for editorial consideration:**

1. **This is a simulation study**, and the manuscript says so in the abstract rather than
   burying it in the limitations. The propagation is ray-traced (Sionna RT); the only
   real-data element is the KITTI LiDAR anchor, which validates the SLAM back-end but not
   the channel model. The most valuable follow-up measurement — confirming the phantom-
   detection rate on real vehicular CSI — is named as such.

2. **Relationship to my prior submission.** This work extends an earlier manuscript of mine
   [cite once published]. That paper is cited as related work only: **every WiFi number in
   this paper is regenerated by this paper's own runs** (reported as mean ± std over five
   seeds), so no claim here depends on a result a reviewer cannot verify from the artifacts
   released with *this* manuscript. Section VIII additionally **refines an interpretation**
   of that earlier work — it attributed the mapping floor to path discrimination, and the
   isolation experiment here shows discrimination is the *smallest* of three mechanisms. I
   state this plainly in the paper rather than quietly diverging from it.

3. **The cost claim is conditional and the condition is stated.** The 84–600× advantage
   rests on treating ambient access points as pre-existing infrastructure. The manuscript
   includes the sensitivity: with three self-deployed APs the WiFi package can become *more
   expensive* than the cheapest solid-state LiDAR. I would rather present the boundary than
   the headline alone.

4. **Prices are dated market estimates**, each carried with its source and a verification
   flag in the released artifact.
